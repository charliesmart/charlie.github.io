Maps Are Lying To You

Chances are, you see maps everywhere. And I'm not just talking about the kind you look at on your phone to help get you from point A to point B. I'm talking about the kind of maps that are used to convey data.

A lot of those maps–the kind you might see in magazines or on the evening news–are probably choropleths. That's the kind of map where regions are colored based on some data.

choropleth  /ˈkɔːrəˌplɛθ/  noun

A map which uses differences in shading, colouring, or the placing of symbols within predefined areas to indicate the average values of a particular quantity in those areas.
